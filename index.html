About Me

I am a Masters student studying operations research at the University of Maryland, and in my (rare) free time, I make pottery. I treasure every hour I can escape the grading, researching, and doing homework to go stick my hands in a mess of clay.
To me, pottery is about sticking your hands in earth, feeling it squish between your fingers, and guiding it into shapes that had perviously existed only in your imagination. And of course, it's always fun to get a little dirty ;)

Research

Using Dynamic Programming to Optimize End-User Electricity Use

Graduate Research Assistant • August 2017 - Present
Advisor: Dr. Steven A. Gabriel

Price volatility in electricity markets can cause Retail Electric Providers significant losses in short amounts of time. Along with our corporate partner Whisker Labs we have been using internet-connected thermostats to remotely shift residential HVAC load from periods of high prices to low prices. My work has two thrusts: first to predict price spikes, and second to optimally shift demand away from high prices. The first step involves calibrating Recurrent Neural Networks to predict energy price spikes for real-time decision making. The second step uses Stochastic Dynamic Programming (SDP) to optimally schedule residential demand response events.

Modeling Harmful Algal Blooms in the Chesapeake Bay

Undergraduate Research Assistant• September 2016 - May 2017
Advisor: Dr. Kaye Brubaker

Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs) pose a threat to both ecosystem and human health. The dangers of these organisms are twofold: many strains produce toxins lethal to fish and harmful to humans, and the decay of algae biomass can cause eutrophication in water bodies. For this project, I first developed a life cycle model of algae bloom probabilities on the Chesapeake Bay. Then, I trained and validated model using cell abundances provided by MD Department of Natural Resources. This project was completed as fufilment of requirements of the Engineering Honors Program and earned the Most Outstanding Research Award among other projects complete that year.
